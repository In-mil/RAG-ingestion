# The History of Spices

## Worth More Than Gold

In medieval Europe, a pound of saffron cost as much as a horse. Peppercorns were so valuable they were used as currency and accepted for rent payments. A dock worker in London could buy a house with a sack of pepper.

## The Spice Trade Shaped the World Map

The desire to find sea routes to spice-producing regions directly led to the Age of Exploration. The search for a western route to the Spice Islands motivated the voyage that resulted in the European discovery of the Americas in 1492.

## Cinnamon's Secret Origin

For centuries, Arab traders kept the source of cinnamon secret, claiming it came from mysterious lands guarded by giant birds. In reality, it grew in Sri Lanka. The deception maintained their monopoly and kept prices high.

## Vanilla Is an Orchid

Vanilla comes from the seed pods of a climbing orchid native to Mexico. The flowers open for only one day and in the wild are pollinated by a single species of bee. Outside Mexico, every vanilla flower must be pollinated by hand, making it the second most expensive spice after saffron.

## Chili Peppers Trick Your Brain

Capsaicin, the compound that makes chili peppers hot, binds to the same pain receptors that detect actual heat. Your brain genuinely believes your mouth is burning. Birds lack this receptor entirely, which is why they eat chili peppers without any reaction.
