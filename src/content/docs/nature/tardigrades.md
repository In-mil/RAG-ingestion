# Tardigrades

## Nearly Indestructible

Tardigrades (water bears) are microscopic animals roughly 0.5 mm long that can survive conditions lethal to virtually all other life forms. They have been found on every continent, from deep ocean trenches to mountaintops.

## What They Survive

- Temperatures from minus 272 degrees Celsius (near absolute zero) to 150 degrees Celsius
- Pressures six times greater than the deepest ocean trench
- Radiation hundreds of times the lethal dose for humans
- The vacuum of outer space (confirmed in 2007 by the TARDIS experiment aboard a satellite)
- Complete dehydration for decades

## Cryptobiosis

When conditions become hostile, tardigrades enter a state called cryptobiosis. They retract their legs, expel nearly all water from their bodies, and curl into a ball called a tun. Metabolism drops to less than 0.01 percent of normal. In this state, they can remain dormant for at least 30 years.

## Moon Crash

In 2019, an Israeli spacecraft carrying dehydrated tardigrades crashed on the Moon. Scientists believe the tardigrades likely survived the impact in their dormant tun state, making them potential (if unintentional) lunar residents.
