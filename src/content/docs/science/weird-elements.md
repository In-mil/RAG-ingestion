# Weird Facts About Chemical Elements

## Gallium Melts in Your Hand

Gallium has a melting point of 29.76 degrees Celsius. Holding a piece in your palm is enough body heat to turn it into a liquid puddle. Despite this, its boiling point is over 2400 degrees Celsius.

## Francium Is Almost Impossible to Study

Francium is the most unstable naturally occurring element. At any given moment, there are only about 20 to 30 grams of it on the entire Earth. Its most stable isotope has a half-life of just 22 minutes.

## Bismuth Forms Rainbow Crystals

When bismuth oxidizes, it creates a thin oxide layer that refracts light into iridescent rainbow colors. The crystals grow in distinctive staircase-shaped hopper structures.

## Helium Cannot Be Frozen at Normal Pressure

No matter how cold you make helium, it remains liquid at standard atmospheric pressure. It only solidifies under pressures of about 25 atmospheres. Below 2.17 Kelvin, liquid helium becomes a superfluid with zero viscosity.
