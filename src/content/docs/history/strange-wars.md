# Strange Wars in History

## The Emu War (1932)

Australia deployed soldiers with Lewis guns against a population of roughly 20,000 emus that were destroying wheat crops in Western Australia. The emus proved surprisingly resilient and evasive. After several failed engagements, the military withdrew. The emus won.

## The War of the Bucket (1325)

The city-states of Modena and Bologna in Italy fought a war that was triggered, at least symbolically, by the theft of a wooden bucket. The Battle of Zappolino involved over 30,000 soldiers. Modena won and kept the bucket. It is still on display in Modena's cathedral tower.

## The Football War (1969)

El Salvador and Honduras fought a four-day war following tensions that escalated during qualifying matches for the 1970 FIFA World Cup. The underlying causes were land reform disputes and migration, but the football matches served as the catalyst. Around 3,000 people died.

## The Cod Wars (1958-1976)

Iceland and the United Kingdom had three separate confrontations over fishing rights in the North Atlantic. Despite being called wars, they involved mostly ramming between coast guard vessels and trawlers. Iceland won each time by threatening to leave NATO, which would have created a gap in Cold War defenses.
