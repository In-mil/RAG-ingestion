# Roman Concrete

## Stronger Than Modern Concrete

Roman concrete structures like the Pantheon have survived over 2000 years. Many modern concrete structures begin to deteriorate after 50 to 100 years. Researchers discovered that Roman concrete actually gets stronger over time when exposed to seawater.

## The Secret Ingredient

The Romans mixed volcanic ash (pozzolana) from the area around Pozzuoli with lime and seawater. When seawater seeps into cracks, it triggers a chemical reaction that creates aluminous tobermorite crystals, which reinforce the material instead of weakening it.

## The Pantheon Dome

The Pantheon in Rome has the largest unreinforced concrete dome in the world, with a diameter of 43.3 meters. It was built around 125 CE and still stands without any steel reinforcement. The Romans made the concrete progressively lighter toward the top by mixing in pumice instead of heavier stone.

## Lost and Rediscovered

The recipe was lost after the fall of the Western Roman Empire. Modern researchers at the University of Utah only began to understand the chemistry in 2017 by examining samples drilled from ancient Roman harbors.
