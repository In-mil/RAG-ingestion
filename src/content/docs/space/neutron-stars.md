# Neutron Stars

## Density Beyond Imagination

A neutron star packs about 1.4 times the mass of the Sun into a sphere roughly 20 kilometers across. A sugar-cube-sized piece of neutron star material would weigh about 1 billion tonnes on Earth.

## Spin Speed

Some neutron stars (pulsars) rotate hundreds of times per second. The fastest known pulsar, PSR J1748-2446ad, spins at 716 revolutions per second. A point on its equator moves at roughly 24 percent the speed of light.

## Magnetic Fields

Magnetars are neutron stars with the strongest magnetic fields in the universe, up to a quadrillion (10 to the power of 15) times stronger than Earth's. At half the distance to the Moon, a magnetar's field would erase every credit card on Earth.

## How They Form

When a massive star (8 to 20 times the mass of the Sun) exhausts its nuclear fuel, its core collapses in milliseconds. Protons and electrons are crushed together into neutrons. The outer layers are blasted away as a supernova while the core compresses into the neutron star.

## Glitches

Neutron stars occasionally speed up suddenly in events called glitches. These are thought to occur when the superfluid interior transfers angular momentum to the rigid crust, causing a tiny but measurable jump in rotation rate.
