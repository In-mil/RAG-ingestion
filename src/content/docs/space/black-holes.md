# Black Hole Facts

## Time Slows Down Near Them

Due to gravitational time dilation, time passes slower the closer you get to a black hole. An observer far away would see someone falling toward the event horizon slow down and eventually appear to freeze, though the falling person would experience time normally.

## The Smallest and Largest

The smallest known black holes are about 3 times the mass of the Sun and roughly 18 kilometers across. The largest known black hole, TON 618, has a mass of 66 billion Suns. Its event horizon is over 390 billion kilometers in diameter.

## Spaghettification

If you fell into a stellar-mass black hole feet first, the gravitational difference between your feet and head would stretch you into a thin strand of matter. Physicists actually call this process spaghettification.

## They Evaporate

Stephen Hawking predicted in 1974 that black holes slowly emit radiation and lose mass over time. A black hole with the mass of the Sun would take roughly 10 to the power of 67 years to evaporate. For comparison, the current age of the universe is about 1.38 times 10 to the power of 10 years.

## Sound From a Black Hole

In 2022, NASA released a sonification of pressure waves emanating from a black hole in the Perseus galaxy cluster. The original sound wave has a frequency of about one cycle every 10 million years, roughly 57 octaves below middle C.
