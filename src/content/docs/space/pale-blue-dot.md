# The Pale Blue Dot

## The Photo

On February 14, 1990, the Voyager 1 spacecraft turned its camera back toward Earth from a distance of about 6 billion kilometers. Earth appears as a tiny speck, less than a single pixel in size, suspended in a beam of scattered sunlight.

## Why It Was Taken

Carl Sagan had campaigned for years to have Voyager photograph Earth from deep space. Many at NASA considered it a waste of resources since the image had no scientific value. Sagan argued its value was philosophical.

## The Distance

At the time the photo was taken, Voyager 1 was beyond the orbit of Pluto. The radio signal carrying the image traveled at the speed of light and took about 5 hours and 36 minutes to reach Earth.

## Where Voyager Is Now

Voyager 1 entered interstellar space in August 2012, becoming the first human-made object to do so. As of 2025, it is over 24 billion kilometers from Earth and still transmitting data, powered by a plutonium-238 radioisotope thermoelectric generator that will likely run out around 2025 to 2030.
